# Build a Target Company List

Use this workflow when you need to identify where relevant talent is likely to come from.

---

## Goal

Create a prioritized list of companies that are likely to employ people with the skills, experience, domain context, or technical environment you need.

---

## Inputs

- Role title
- Must-have skills
- Nice-to-have skills
- Industry/domain
- Location constraints
- Seniority level
- Target companies already known
- Companies to avoid
- Hiring manager context

---

## Workflow

1. Start with direct competitors.
2. Add adjacent industry companies.
3. Add companies using similar technologies.
4. Add companies hiring similar roles.
5. Add suppliers, customers, partners, or ecosystem companies.
6. Add companies with relevant product complexity.
7. Tag each company by relevance and confidence.
8. Prioritize based on talent likelihood, brand relevance, location, and recruiting feasibility.

---

## Suggested columns

| Column | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Company | Company name |
| Category | Competitor, adjacent, customer, supplier, ecosystem, etc. |
| Why relevant | Why this company belongs on the list |
| Skills likely present | Skills or domains likely to exist there |
| Locations | Relevant offices or talent hubs |
| Priority | High, medium, low |
| Notes | Context, caveats, links, or recruiter observations |

---

## Output

A target company list recruiters can use for sourcing, talent mapping, market research, and hiring manager calibration.
