# Map a Talent Market

Use this workflow when you need to understand the size, shape, location, and competitiveness of a talent pool.

---

## Goal

Turn scattered market signals into a practical recruiting view of where talent exists, how accessible it may be, and what risks the hiring team needs to understand.

---

## Inputs

- Role title
- Required skills
- Adjacent skills
- Target level
- Location requirements
- Compensation range if available
- Competitor list
- Hiring timeline

---

## Workflow

1. Define the role in plain language.
2. Translate requirements into searchable titles and skills.
3. Identify target companies and adjacent industries.
4. Estimate geographic talent concentration.
5. Review competitor hiring activity.
6. Identify title variation and skill adjacency.
7. Note market risks and constraints.
8. Summarize what recruiting should do next.

---

## Suggested output sections

- Market summary
- Talent pool notes
- Likely source companies
- Location insights
- Search risks
- Messaging angles
- Recommended next steps

---

## Practical rule

A talent market map should help someone make a decision. If it only collects facts, it is research. If it explains what the facts mean, it is intelligence.
