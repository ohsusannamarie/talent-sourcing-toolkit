# Write Better Outreach

Use this workflow when drafting candidate messages that are specific, respectful, and actually readable.

---

## Goal

Write outreach that gives the candidate a clear reason to care without sounding like a bulk email wearing a fake mustache.

---

## Inputs

- Candidate context
- Role context
- Company context
- Why this person may be relevant
- Why the opportunity may be relevant to them
- One clear call to action

---

## Workflow

1. Identify the actual reason you are reaching out.
2. Find one relevant professional signal.
3. Connect that signal to the role.
4. Keep the message short.
5. Remove hype, filler, and fake enthusiasm.
6. Make the ask easy to answer.
7. Add a thoughtful follow-up if needed.

---

## Message structure

```txt
Hi [Name],

I came across your work/background in [specific relevant area]. I am working on a [role/team/problem] where that experience looks relevant, especially [specific connection].

Would you be open to a quick conversation about it?
```

---

## Avoid

- “I was impressed by your profile” with no reason why
- Long company pitches before explaining relevance
- Fake personalization
- Overly casual forced energy
- Vague roles
- Seven-paragraph life stories

---

## Practical rule

The candidate should understand three things fast: why them, why this, and what you want next.
