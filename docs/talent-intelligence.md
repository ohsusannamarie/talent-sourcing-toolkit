# Talent Intelligence Guide

Talent intelligence turns messy market signals into useful recruiting strategy.

Use this section for market mapping, competitor research, location strategy, skill supply analysis, compensation context, hiring feasibility, and leadership-ready insights.

---

## What talent intelligence helps answer

- Where does this talent actually sit?
- Which companies employ people with these skills?
- What job titles are adjacent or equivalent?
- Which locations have enough talent supply?
- Which competitors are hiring similar people?
- What skills are core vs adjacent?
- Is the hiring plan realistic?
- What story should recruiting tell leadership?

---

## Core research categories

### Company mapping

Use this to identify source companies, adjacent employers, competitors, suppliers, customers, and ecosystem players.

Look for:

- Direct competitors
- Adjacent industry employers
- Companies using similar technology
- Companies hiring similar roles
- Companies with relevant products, customers, or technical environments

### Talent pool analysis

Use this to understand where qualified people are, what titles they use, and how dense the market is.

Look for:

- Title variations
- Skill clusters
- Geographic concentration
- Seniority distribution
- Common career paths
- Talent movement patterns

### Skill adjacency

Use this to expand a search without watering it down.

Look for:

- Similar tools
- Related technologies
- Prior-generation platforms
- Adjacent engineering domains
- Transferable technical environments

### Hiring feasibility

Use this to tell the truth about the market.

Look for:

- Talent supply vs hiring demand
- Compensation competitiveness
- Location constraints
- Remote/hybrid/on-site friction
- Seniority scarcity
- Brand awareness challenges

## Talent intelligence output formats

### Quick brief

Best for fast updates.

Include:

- Market summary
- Talent pool notes
- Top source companies
- Search risks
- Recommended next steps

### Talent map

Best for sourcers and recruiters.

Include:

- Target companies
- Titles
- Skills
- Locations
- Notes
- Search strings
- Priority levels

### Leadership insight memo

Best for hiring leaders.

Include:

- Key finding
- Why it matters
- Evidence
- Risk
- Recommendation
- Decision needed

---

## Starter research workflow

1. Define the role and must-have skills.
2. Translate job requirements into searchable language.
3. Identify likely source companies.
4. Map title variations and skill adjacencies.
5. Check location constraints and talent density.
6. Look for active hiring demand from competitors.
7. Summarize risks and recommendations.
8. Turn findings into a recruiter-ready or leader-ready artifact.

---

## Practical rule

Talent intelligence is not just data collection.

The value is interpretation: what the market is telling you, what decision it supports, and what recruiting should do next.
