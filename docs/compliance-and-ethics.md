# Compliance and Ethics Notes

This is not legal advice. It is a practical reminder that sourcing tools, AI tools, browser extensions, enrichment platforms, and recruiting workflows can create privacy, security, fairness, and compliance concerns.

Use curiosity and judgment.

---

## Watch-outs for recruiters and sourcers

### Candidate data

Be careful with tools that collect, enrich, export, store, or sync candidate information.

Ask:

- What data is being collected?
- Where is it stored?
- Who can access it?
- Does your company allow this tool?
- Is the data source clear?
- Can candidates opt out or request deletion?

### Browser extensions

Browser extensions can be powerful, but they can also see more than you expect.

Check:

- Requested permissions
- Update history
- Developer credibility
- Privacy policy
- Reviews
- Whether it reads or changes site data
- Whether it interacts with email, LinkedIn, cookies, contacts, or candidate records

### AI tools

AI tools can help with research, writing, summarization, and search strategy.

Be careful when AI is used to:

- Score candidates
- Rank candidates
- Screen resumes
- Recommend who should move forward
- Reject candidates
- Infer protected or sensitive traits
- Make decisions without human review

### Public information

Public does not automatically mean appropriate.

Use public data responsibly, avoid creepy personalization, and do not overreach into irrelevant personal details.

---

## Responsible sourcing principles

- Be transparent when appropriate.
- Personalize based on professional relevance.
- Avoid sensitive personal details.
- Validate information before acting on it.
- Follow company policy.
- Respect candidate privacy.
- Use AI as support, not authority.
- Treat tools as helpers, not shortcuts around judgment.

---

## Practical rule

If you would not be comfortable explaining the workflow to a candidate, manager, legal partner, or security team, pause before using it.
