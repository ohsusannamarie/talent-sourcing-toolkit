# AI Recruiting Guide

AI can help recruiters move faster, but it should not replace judgment, accountability, or candidate care.

Use this guide as a practical starting point for AI-supported sourcing, outreach, intake, research, and workflow design.

---

## Useful AI recruiting use cases

### Search strategy

Use AI to:

- Expand Boolean strings
- Generate alternate job titles
- Identify skill adjacencies
- Translate hiring manager language into searchable keywords
- Create X-ray search variations
- Build company and talent market hypotheses

### Intake and role analysis

Use AI to:

- Summarize intake notes
- Extract must-haves vs nice-to-haves
- Flag ambiguous requirements
- Create candidate calibration questions
- Translate technical requirements into recruiter-friendly language

### Outreach support

Use AI to:

- Draft first-pass outreach
- Rewrite messages in a more human tone
- Create role-specific personalization angles
- Summarize why a role may appeal to a candidate
- Generate follow-up variations

### Talent intelligence

Use AI to:

- Summarize company research
- Compare talent markets
- Identify likely source companies
- Create interview prep briefs
- Turn raw research into leader-ready insights

### Recruiter productivity

Use AI to:

- Summarize notes
- Create checklists
- Generate templates
- Organize messy research
- Draft status updates
- Build reusable workflows

---

## Caution zones

Be careful with tools or workflows that:

- Score candidates
- Rank candidates
- Recommend who should move forward
- Reject candidates automatically
- Infer sensitive traits
- Use unclear data sources
- Enrich personal data without clear policy coverage
- Make hiring decisions without meaningful human review

AI should support decisions, not secretly make them.

## Better AI prompts for recruiters

### Boolean expansion

```txt
Act as a technical sourcer. Expand this search strategy for a [ROLE] requiring [SKILLS]. Provide title synonyms, skill synonyms, adjacent technologies, exclusion terms, and 5 Boolean string variations.
```

### Intake cleanup

```txt
Summarize these intake notes into must-haves, nice-to-haves, dealbreakers, unclear requirements, candidate selling points, and follow-up questions for the hiring manager.
```

### Outreach rewrite

```txt
Rewrite this candidate outreach message to sound more direct, human, and specific. Keep it under 120 words. Avoid hype, fake personalization, and corporate filler.
```

### Market research synthesis

```txt
Turn these raw market research notes into a concise talent intelligence brief for recruiting leadership. Include key findings, risks, recommended source companies, and next steps.
```

---

## Practical rule

If the AI output would embarrass you if a candidate, hiring manager, or compliance partner saw it, do not use it as-is.

Edit. Verify. Own the final work.
