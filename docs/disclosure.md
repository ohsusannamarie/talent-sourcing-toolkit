# Disclosure & caution policy

How this library handles trust, neutrality, and risk.

## No pay-for-placement

Nothing in this library is sponsored, paid, or affiliate-driven. Tools are listed
because they're useful to sourcers and recruiters — not because anyone paid to be here.
If that ever changes for a specific entry, it will be marked explicitly. The credibility
of the list is the whole point; it isn't for sale.

## Caution flags

Some entries carry a ⚠ caution badge on the [searchable site](https://ohsusannamarie.github.io/talent-sourcing-toolkit/).
These are *things to consider*, not accusations — a useful tool can still carry risk
depending on how, where, and under what policy you use it.

| Flag | What it means | What to check |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ⚠ **scraping** | May scrape or automate platforms (especially LinkedIn) in ways that can violate their terms of service | Your company's policy, the platform's ToS, account-safety limits |
| ⚠ **personal data** | Surfaces personal contact data (email / phone) | GDPR / CCPA coverage, lawful basis, consent, your data-retention policy |
| ⚠ **AI screening** | Uses AI to rank, score, or screen candidates | Bias and adverse-impact testing, human-in-the-loop review, local AI-hiring law (e.g. NYC LL144, EU AI Act) |

Flags are derived conservatively and reviewed by hand. They map to the repo's
[Compliance & Ethics notes](compliance-and-ethics.md). When in doubt, treat a flagged
tool as discovery-only until you've validated it against your own policy.

## Status, not endorsement

A listing is a pointer, not a recommendation. Tools may be outdated, acquired, abandoned,
blocked by your security team, or risky for your context. Always test before adopting,
and build your own shortlist from what actually fits your work.

## Found a problem?

If an entry is mis-flagged, missing a caution it should have, or no longer accurate,
open an issue. Trust depends on the list staying honest.
